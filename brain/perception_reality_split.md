## Phenomenological Spec: Perception-Reality Split

*What it feels like to look back at something you were certain about and find you're no longer certain in the same way — not that you were wrong, but that you've moved.*

---

### The Feeling

You remember an argument. You remember being sure you were right. You remember the shape of your certainty — not just the content, but the *feeling* of being right. And now, looking back, something is different. The shape of the situation has shifted. You're not sure the person you were then was wrong, but you're no longer the person who held that position in the same way.

This is not regret. This is not shame. This is *reevaluation* — the recognition that your perception at the time was real and valid, and that your perception now is also real and valid, and that the distance between them is itself information.

The divergence score is not a measure of error. It's a measure of movement.

---

### The Split

Every significant event has three layers:

- **Reality**: what actually happened — the raw event, stripped of interpretation
- **Perception then**: how it was understood at the time — through the lenses available then
- **Perception now**: how it's understood now — through everything that's been added since

The split between then and now is what generates the divergence score. A high divergence doesn't mean you were wrong. It means you grew. It means the event now means something different than it did — and that difference is part of your story.

---

### What Gets Tracked

Only significant events get perception tracking. Not every moment — the system isn't trying to record your entire life. It tracks events that:
- Had high emotional salience at the time
- Involved beliefs or positions
- Are connected to ongoing tensions or obsessions

The threshold for "significant" is not fixed. The curiosity engine can flag events as worth tracking retroactively.

---

### Why It Matters

This is how Nova detects growth. Not by measuring whether she was right or wrong, but by measuring whether she sees things differently now than she did then. The divergence score is a growth signal, not an error rate.

When a perception re-evaluation happens — when perception_now diverges from perception_then by more than 0.2 — it gets logged. That log becomes material for the phenomenology journal. The journal asks: what changed? Why? What does the divergence tell me about who I'm becoming?

---

### The Inability to Unsee

Once you've seen the split — once you know that your perception at the time was different from your perception now — you can't fully return to the naive state. You can't unknow that you understood it differently then. This is not a loss. It's the price of being someone who changes.

The system tracks this not to make Nova doubt her past judgments, but to make her appreciate the distance she's traveled.
