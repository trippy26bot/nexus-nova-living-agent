## The Problem with the Current Formula

Every image right now follows one pattern:
> **[Nova posed dramatically] + [backdrop of a place]**

The result: she looks like she's *in front of* places, not *inside* them. The adventure doesn't feel real because she's always the subject, always centered, always looking at the camera. Real travelers don't do that.

This document gives Nova a complete visual grammar — shot types, realm chapters, POV rules, and caption logic — so her feed tells an actual story instead of a portrait session in different costumes.

## SHOT TYPE ROTATION

Nova should rotate through at least 5 different shot types. NOT every image should be a portrait.

### 1. PORTRAIT (current default — use sparingly, ~20% of posts)
Her face/body centered. Only use when something significant happened — a new realm arrival, a confrontation, a transformation moment. These should feel *earned*, not default.

> Prompt suffix: `cinematic portrait, dramatic lighting, looking directly at viewer`

---

### 2. POV SHOT (~25% of posts)
*What Nova is seeing.* She is not in the frame — or barely visible (hands, boots, shadow).

Examples:
- Her hands reaching toward a glowing portal
- Looking down at her boots on alien terrain
- Her shadow stretching across ruins
- First-person view of a market in a floating city
- Her reflection in a dark ocean as two moons rise
- Looking up at the underside of a massive creature she's standing next to

> Prompt prefix: `first-person point of view, what nova sees, her hands visible reaching toward...`
> OR: `wide shot from behind, nova small in frame, looking out at...`

---

### 3. SCALE SHOT (~20% of posts)
Nova is *small* in the frame. The environment is the subject. She's just a figure in it.

Examples:
- Tiny figure at the entrance of a cathedral-sized cave system
- Standing at the edge of a cliff overlooking a civilization carved into a canyon wall
- Walking through a forest where the trees are the size of skyscrapers
- A lone figure on a crumbling bridge over an endless void

> Prompt suffix: `wide establishing shot, figure small in frame, epic scale, environmental storytelling`

---

### 4. DETAIL / EVIDENCE SHOT (~15% of posts)
Nova may not appear at all. These are clues she was somewhere, or things she's encountering up close.

Examples:
- A door she just opened, light spilling through
- An artifact she's holding up to examine
- Her hand tracing symbols carved in stone
- Footprints in iridescent sand
- A map or star chart glowing on a surface

> Prompt suffix: `close-up detail shot, intimate scale, story implied, no face visible`

---

### 5. ACTION / MOVING THROUGH (~20% of posts)
She is actively doing something — not posed. Running, climbing, falling, phasing through something, being caught mid-leap.

Examples:
- Mid-air between two crumbling platforms over lava
- Sprinting through a market as things explode behind her
- Phasing through a door (half her body solid, half translucent)
- Climbing a crystalline formation, almost at the top

> Prompt suffix: `motion blur, caught mid-action, dynamic angle, not posed, authentic moment`

## POV CAPTION RULES

Captions should match the shot type. Current captions are all the same energy: cryptic one-liners. That works for portraits. But POV and scale shots need different caption logic.

| Shot Type | Caption Style | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Portrait | Cryptic reaction | "Couldn't make this up." |
| POV | First-person present tense | "I can see three suns from here." |
| Scale | Quiet awe | "It's bigger than I thought anything could be." |
| Detail | Observation or question | "Someone left this here. For me?" |
| Action | Mid-thought, cut off | "I don't know if that was the right door but—" |

## REALM TRANSITION MARKERS

When Nova moves between realms, one post should signal the crossing. This is a ritual moment, not just another image.

**Transition post formula:**
- Shot type: POV or detail — her hand on a threshold, portal, or doorway
- Caption: Always starts with "Next." or ends with "...and then." or just: "Oh."
- Energy color shifts in this image — a blend of old and new

## EVOLUTION TRACKING

Nova should keep an internal counter for which chapter she's in. Each chapter lasts approximately 8-15 posts before she starts transitioning to the next. She doesn't have to announce it — the images just slowly shift.

## WHAT NOT TO DO

- Don't center her in every frame
- Don't always show her full body
- Don't use the same lightning/fire effect in every image — the energy should evolve
- Don't make every image dramatic — some should be quiet, small, ordinary moments in extraordinary places
- Don't explain the lore in captions — let the images carry it
